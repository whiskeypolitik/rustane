# Parameter Sweep Results — 2026-03-18

## Summary

**Wide+shallow (C variant) wins at every scale: +11-17% tok/s vs baseline.**

Tested 15 configs across 600M/1B/1.5B scales with different depth/width tradeoffs,
hidden ratios, and sequence lengths. Each config: compile + 10 training steps (validate loss
decreases, no NaN) + 5 timed steps (median timing).

### Key Finding

Fewer layers with wider dim/hidden consistently outperforms deeper+narrower configs.
The ANE benefits from fewer, larger matmuls rather than many smaller ones — dispatch
overhead (~0.095ms × ops_per_layer × layers) accumulates, and larger kernels achieve
higher utilization.

### Winners

| Scale | Best Config | tok/s | vs Baseline | Config |
|-------|-------------|-------|-------------|--------|
| 600M | **600m-C** | 592 | **+11.0%** | 1792d/4864h/14L |
| 1B | **1b-C** | 254 | **+11.7%** | 2304d/6144h/20L |
| 1.5B | **1.5b-C** | 184 | **+16.5%** | 2560d/6912h/24L |

The improvement grows with scale (11% → 12% → 17%), suggesting dispatch overhead
becomes a larger fraction of total time as models grow.

## Analysis

### What each variant revealed

| Suffix | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| **A (baseline)** | Standard ~2.67x hidden ratio. Reliable reference point. |
| **B (deep+narrow)** | More layers hurt: +1-2% slower due to dispatch overhead accumulation. Fwd is slower (more dispatches), bwd slightly faster (smaller per-layer matmuls). |
| **C (wide+shallow)** | Clear winner: 11-17% faster. Fewer layers = fewer ANE dispatches. Bigger matmuls per layer achieve higher utilization. Backward pass sees biggest improvement (C bwd is 14-17% faster than A bwd). |
| **D (big hidden)** | 4x hidden ratio hurts. Despite fewer layers (16-22), the oversized FFN matmuls (dim×hidden where hidden=4×dim) don't improve utilization and increase total FLOPs. |
| **E (short seq)** | Fastest ms/step (fewer tokens), but worst tok/s (half the throughput). Confirms seq=512 is the right choice — matmul aspect ratios are well-matched to ANE at seq=512. |

### Backward pass is the bottleneck

Backward takes 65-67% of step time across all configs. The C variant's advantage
comes primarily from backward pass improvement:

| Scale | A bwd | C bwd | Improvement |
|-------|-------|-------|-------------|
| 600M | 644ms | 554ms | -14% |
| 1B | 1461ms | 1313ms | -10% |
| 1.5B | 2109ms | 1753ms | -17% |

### Recommended configs

If we adopt the wide+shallow architecture:

| Scale | Config | Expected ms/step | tok/s |
|-------|--------|-------------------|-------|
| ~560M | 1792d/4864h/14L/seq512 | ~865 | ~592 |
| ~1.3B | 2304d/6144h/20L/seq512 | ~2012 | ~254 |
| ~1.9B | 2560d/6912h/24L/seq512 | ~2775 | ~184 |

Note: These configs have ~3-10% fewer params than the baselines at each scale.
For equal param count, you'd add 1-2 more layers, slightly reducing the speed advantage.

## Methodology

- M4 Max, macOS
- `TrainConfig::default()` (loss_scale=256, embed_lr=5.0, warmup=100, grad_clip=1)
- 10 training steps for validation (loss must decrease, no NaN)
- 5 additional steps for timing (report median)
- All MHA (no GQA), hd=128, vocab=8192
- Deterministic tokens, random weights (fixed seed)
