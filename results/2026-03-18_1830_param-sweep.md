# Parameter Sweep Results — 2026-03-18

## Summary

**25 configs across 5 scales (600M → 5B). All compile, train, and fit on M4 Max 128GB.**

The optimal architecture flips with scale:
- **600M–1.5B**: wide+shallow (C) wins (+11-17%), dispatch overhead dominates
- **3B**: crossover — baseline (A) is fastest
- **5B**: deep+narrow (B) wins (+16%), per-matmul compute dominates

### Winners by Scale

| Scale | Best Config | tok/s | ms/step | vs Baseline | Why |
|-------|-------------|-------|---------|-------------|-----|
| 600M | **600m-C** (wide+shallow) | 592 | 865ms | **+11.0%** | Fewer dispatches |
| 1B | **1b-C** (wide+shallow) | 254 | 2.0s | **+11.7%** | Fewer dispatches |
| 1.5B | **1.5b-C** (wide+shallow) | 184 | 2.8s | **+16.5%** | Fewer dispatches |
| 3B | **3b-A** (baseline) | 110 | 4.6s | — | Crossover point |
| 5B | **5b-B** (deep+narrow) | 74 | 6.9s | **+15.6%** | Smaller matmuls faster |

### Architecture–Scale Crossover

At small scales, ANE dispatch overhead (~0.095ms × ops × layers) is a large fraction
of step time. Fewer layers with bigger matmuls wins. At large scales (dim≥3072),
per-matmul compute dominates and smaller, more numerous matmuls are more efficient.

The crossover happens around **3B params / dim≈2560**. Below that, go wide+shallow.
Above that, go deep+narrow.

## Analysis

### Architecture–scale crossover explained

| Scale | Winner | dim | layers | Explanation |
|-------|--------|-----|--------|-------------|
| 600M | C (14L) | 1792 | 14 | 6 fewer layers × ~0.1ms dispatch × ~10 ops = saves ~6ms/step |
| 1B | C (20L) | 2304 | 20 | Same effect, 8 fewer layers |
| 1.5B | C (24L) | 2560 | 24 | Peak advantage (+17%), dispatch overhead is largest fraction |
| 3B | A (40L) | 2560 | 40 | Crossover — dispatch and compute roughly balanced |
| 5B | B (60L) | 2560 | 60 | dim=3072 matmuls are so large they're less efficient on ANE |

The crossover at dim≈2560–3072 suggests the ANE's optimal matmul size is in this range.
Larger dims hit diminishing returns in hardware utilization.

### 5b-D forward pass anomaly

5b-D (4x hidden ratio, hidden=12288) has an abnormally slow forward pass (4636ms vs
~2000ms for others). The FFN matmuls at dim=3072 × hidden=12288 create IOSurfaces
of ~192MB each, possibly exceeding efficient ANE transfer bandwidth.

### What each variant revealed (across all scales)

| Suffix | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| **A (baseline)** | Standard ~2.67x hidden ratio. Reliable reference. Best at 3B. |
| **B (deep+narrow)** | Hurts at small scale (dispatch overhead), wins at 5B (smaller efficient matmuls). |
| **C (wide+shallow)** | Wins 600M–1.5B (+11-17%). Loses at 5B — very large matmuls hit ANE efficiency ceiling. |
| **D (big hidden)** | Worst or near-worst at every scale. 4x hidden ratio is always suboptimal. Especially bad at 5B where fwd is 2x slower. |
| **E (short seq)** | Always fastest ms/step, always worst tok/s. Confirms seq=512 is correct. |

### Backward pass dominates

Backward takes 63-67% of step time at all scales:

| Scale | fwd % | bwd % | upd % |
|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| 600M | 24% | 67% | 6% |
| 1.5B | 27% | 65% | 6% |
| 3B | 29% | 63% | 6% |
| 5B | 26% | 67% | 6% |

## Methodology

- M4 Max 128GB, macOS
- `TrainConfig::default()` (loss_scale=256, embed_lr=5.0, warmup=100, grad_clip=1)
- 10 training steps for validation (loss must decrease, no NaN)
- 5 additional steps for timing (report median)
- All MHA (no GQA), hd=128, vocab=8192
- Deterministic tokens, random weights (fixed seed)
